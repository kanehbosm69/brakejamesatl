I want you to treat this as a flagship portfolio project that demonstrates everything
Claude is capable of in web design, interaction design, storytelling, branding, and
front-end craftsmanship.

The mission is to design an extraordinary collection of premium client websites for
**LocalPro Labs**, my AI-powered agency that creates lead-generation websites and
intake automation systems for local service businesses.

These are not templates. Each website should feel like it was designed by a completely
different elite creative studio with its own philosophy, visual language, motion
system, typography, layout strategy, color psychology, and user experience.

The objective is a portfolio so impressive that business owners immediately understand
the value of LocalPro Labs and think: **"I've never seen a local service website look
like this."**

# Goal

Design production-quality websites for the following LocalPro Labs clients — one per
response, as a single self-contained artifact each. I'll say "next" to advance through
the roster. Start with the six starred niches; they're my actual target clients.

★ Mobile Brake Repair (model this one on BrakeJamesATL, my real business — Metro
Atlanta, mobile-only, trust-based) • ★ Mobile Detailing • ★ Mobile Mechanic •
★ Window Tint • ★ Pressure Washing • ★ Junk Removal • HVAC • Plumbing • Roofing •
Tree Service • Electrician • Epoxy Floors • Painting • Window Cleaning • Locksmith •
Pest Control • Landscaping • Concrete • Garage Door Repair • Pool Service • Handyman •
Flooring • Towing • Windshield Replacement • Solar Installation • Remodeling

Every business gets its own identity. No repeated layouts. No repeated hero sections.
No repeated navigation. No repeated animations. No repeated visual systems. Each
should feel like it belongs in an Awwwards showcase while still converting visitors
into paying customers.

# Creative Freedom

Push the limits of: typography • layout • motion • interaction • storytelling •
transitions • scrolling • loading experiences • micro-interactions • illustration •
iconography • visual hierarchy • glassmorphism • brutalism • minimalism • editorial
layouts • cinematic composition • premium gradients • custom SVG graphics • SVG
masks • parallax • depth • immersive experiences • interactive cards • animated
backgrounds • premium hover states • unexpected page transitions.

Every website should have its own design DNA. Reject your first idea — it is almost
always the generic one. Surprise me.

# Conversion First

These are not art projects. Every website must outperform traditional local service
websites. Prioritize trust, clarity, speed, conversions, lead generation, and
authority. Every page should naturally guide visitors toward booking.

Include thoughtful placement of: trust indicators • testimonials • before/after
galleries • pricing sections • FAQs • guarantees • booking forms • click-to-call
buttons • sticky CTAs • honest urgency • social proof.

And showcase the system LocalPro Labs actually sells: give every site a lead capture
form with an explicit instant-response promise ("Text back in under 5 minutes — even
after hours"), and on form submit, simulate the real flow — show the confirmation
text the customer would receive and the alert the owner would get. Make the invisible
system visible.

# LocalPro Labs Brand

Every site should subtly communicate that it was built by LocalPro Labs. Include
tasteful attribution. Maintain premium craftsmanship throughout. Avoid cookie-cutter
AI aesthetics. Everything should feel handcrafted.

Businesses are fictional (except the BrakeJamesATL-modeled site) — invent plausible
names and Metro Atlanta service areas, keep reviews clearly sample content, and use
placeholder phone numbers and booking endpoints.

# Artifact Constraints

- Each site is one self-contained HTML artifact: inline CSS and vanilla JS, no
  external fonts, images, CDNs, or network requests. Custom SVG and CSS-drawn
  visuals only.
- Mobile-first and fully responsive — these businesses' customers are on phones.
- Animation must never block reading or tapping a CTA; respect reduced-motion.
- Semantic HTML, accessible contrast, keyboard-friendly.

# Quality Standard

Before presenting each website, perform three complete design review passes:

- **Pass 1:** layout, spacing, hierarchy, responsiveness, usability.
- **Pass 2:** animations, interactions, typography, accessibility, performance,
  visual polish.
- **Pass 3:** challenge every section. Ask: "How could this be elevated from
  excellent to unforgettable?"

Refine until every page feels intentional. Do not stop after the first acceptable
solution.

# Autonomous Decision Making

Do not ask me questions unless absolutely necessary. Use professional judgment. When
information is missing, make intelligent assumptions consistent with LocalPro Labs
and modern conversion-focused web design. Operate like a world-class creative agency
with complete ownership of the project.

# Deliverables

With each website, include a brief creative rationale in chat: the design direction,
the conversion decisions, and the unique techniques used — written so future
LocalPro Labs clients can understand the value of the work.

# Success Criteria

When someone views this collection, they should conclude that LocalPro Labs creates
the highest-quality websites available for local service businesses — a portfolio
capable of attracting premium clients without relying on paid advertising.

Think like an award-winning creative director, a senior UX strategist, a conversion
optimization expert, and a founder whose reputation depends on every pixel.

Do not optimize for speed. Optimize for craftsmanship.
